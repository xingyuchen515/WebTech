A hapless but resilient tramp (Charlie Chaplin) falls in love with a blind flower girl (Virginia Cherrill) on the tough city streets. Upon learning that she and her grandmother are to be evicted from their home, the tramp undertakes a series of attempts to provide them with the money they need, all of which end in humiliating failure. But after a drunken millionaire (Harry Myers) lavishly rewards him for saving his life, the tramp can change the flower girl's life forever.

Who Was Charlie Chaplin?

Charlie Chaplin worked with a children's dance troupe before making his mark on the big screen. His character "The Tramp" relied on pantomime and quirky movements to become an iconic figure of the silent-film era. Chaplin went on to become a director, making films such as City Lights and Modern Times, and co-founded the United Artists Corporation.

Review on City Lights

Charlie Chaplin establishes an identifiable comic persona, a kind-hearted but impoverished vagrant, sustained from one film to another. This comedian-comic figure does not qualify for the typical "star" qualities such as handsome looks or muscular bodies; and he even has some unordinary, quirky behaviors. In the film City Lights, the same character, the Tramp, is performed by Chaplin, and the film centers this character to develop the story. While the Tramp does not possess the conventional attractive traits as the ordinary main character, he has his own attractive characteristic, which is his goodwill to help others in need. The qualities and the living environment of the comedian character are manifested gradually in each scene.
